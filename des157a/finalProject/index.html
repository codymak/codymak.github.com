Enjoy Life One Teaspoon at a Time

It was a sunny day in April. Flowers were blossoming, and what better way to celebrate than to drink a cup of boba tea. I ordered a Kiwi lime refresher with lychee jellies. It was so refreshing and peaceful to sip on this drink while my friend and I sat down at a park. Of course, I tried dressing up nicely on that day because it was a really beautiful day with bright blue skies and cool breezes. As we talked and talked, we wondered why everything is going by very fast. We talked about how we simply do not have that much time anymore to enjoy peaceful days like this.


We looked at our cup and it was written with the text "Enjoy Life One Teaspoon at a Time. I find it vert charming to see a boba cup giving a really nice advice. It's true that we someties have to just savor the beauty of life slowly. Things are going quickly, so we have to make efforts to do things that we love while we can.

Orange Ramune

Psst...what's that sound? The sound of an ice cold orange ramune soda. It's the third day of my trip in LA. My last day in LA before I go back to Davis. My friends and I woke up extra early to go to Santa Monica's beach. It was a really bright and sunny day. It was peaceful because there weren't a lot of people there. Dear readers, don't make fun of me but I have never been to a sunny beach before. The times that I did, it was always gray and...polluted. But here, at this moment, everything was so blue and vibrant.


I cracked open an orange ramune drink and sat there with my friends, talking about life things and what not. The artificial orange flavor was delightfully enhanced by the salty ocean air. As I sipped my final carbonated drink on the beach, I let out my last breath of air, and went back to the car to say goodbye to LA.

Expensive Wine

It's time to celebrate my friend's successful job in San Francisco. He works in a Three Michelin Star restaruant branch in San Francisco. He invited me over to San Francisco to have dinner with him. At first, there was absolutely no way I would ever pay for an expensive meal...ever. But, he insisted on paying for my meal and wanted to celebrate with me. So, I took the flix bus over to San Francisco, anxious about San Francisco expensive prices.


As we browsed through the menu, I chose the cheapest meals because I really did not have enough money to pay for this meal. My friend told me not to worry, and he treated me to dinner. He ordered a Red Wine to celebrate our friendship together, and to celebrate his new life in San Francisco. The wine tasted different... immensely different than your typical grocery store wine. It tasted sweet, and rich in flavor. A perfect drink to liven the mood, and to let my anxious money worries go away. It was the perfect drink to remember that day.
